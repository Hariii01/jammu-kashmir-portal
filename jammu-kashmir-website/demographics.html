Population Overview

Jammu and Kashmir (the union territory formed after the 2019 reorganisation) is governed as a Union Territory with a legislature. The region is divided into 20 districts across the Jammu and Kashmir divisions.

The Line of Control separates the Indian-administered territory from areas administered by Pakistan (Azad Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan) in the west and north respectively. The region is divided into two divisions and 20 districts.

Religion & Communities

The religious composition varies significantly by division and district:

🕌 Kashmir Division

Predominantly Muslim. The Kashmir Valley has a large Muslim majority, with Kashmiri Pandits (Hindus) as a significant minority — many of whom migrated during the 1990s insurgency.

🛕 Jammu Division

Mixed — Hindu majority in many districts (Jammu, Samba, Kathua), Muslim majority in others (Rajouri, Poonch), Sikh communities in urban centres.

Notable communities include:

  • Kashmiri Pandits: The Hindu Brahmin community indigenous to the Kashmir Valley; many displaced since the 1990s; a politically and culturally significant group.
  • Gujjars and Bakarwals: Nomadic and semi-nomadic pastoralist communities; Scheduled Tribe status; practice transhumance across the mountains.
  • Dogra Rajputs: The ruling community of the former princely state; concentrated in the Jammu region.
