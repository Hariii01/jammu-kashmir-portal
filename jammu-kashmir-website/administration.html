📋 Administration

Administration & Governance

The structure and functioning of the Union Territory of Jammu & Kashmir — its Lieutenant Governor, institutions, divisions, and legal framework after the 2019 reorganisation.

Post-2019 Administrative Framework

After the 2019 reorganisation, Jammu & Kashmir became a Union Territory (UT) with provision for a legislative assembly and a centrally appointed Lieutenant Governor. This changed the governance structure from that of a full state (with a governor and elected state government) to a UT arrangement more tightly integrated with the central government.

Top-Level Administration & Executive Division (2026)

The executive power of the Union Territory of Jammu & Kashmir is divided between the centrally appointed Lieutenant Governor and the elected Chief Minister leading the Council of Ministers.

Executive Function Elected Cabinet (CM Omar Abdullah) Federal Oversight (Lieutenant Governor)
Public Order & Police None. Can raise issues in Assembly but holds no administrative command. Full Control. Manages the J&K Police, security deployments, intelligence, and anti-terror coordination.
Land Administration Advisory role only. Full Control. Manages state land, approvals for industrial parks, and land acquisitions for central projects.
Bureaucracy (Services) None. Civil servants (IAS/IPS) report ultimately to the LG's office. Full Control. Exercises power over appointments, postings, transfers, and disciplinary actions of All-India Services.
Socio-Economic Policy Execution Power. Directs departments including Tourism, Forestry, Public Health, Education, and Agriculture. Has final budget approval and review power on all policies with security or order implications.

Judicial and Legal Framework

  • High Court of Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh: The principal judicial organ of the UT, with seats in Srinagar (summer) and Jammu (winter). Its rulings can be appealed to the Supreme Court of India.
  • Applicability of Central Laws: Following the 2019 reorganisation, the J&K State Penal Code (Ranbir Penal Code) was replaced by the Indian Penal Code (now Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita), and all federal acts passed by Parliament apply directly to the UT.
  • Advocate General & Law Officers: Appointed directly by the Lieutenant Governor under the modified 2024 transaction of business rules, bypassing the Chief Minister's recommendation.

Local Bodies & Urban Governance

  • Municipal Corporations: Srinagar and Jammu Municipal Corporations — responsible for urban civic services.
  • Panchayati Raj Institutions: Three-tier panchayat system (Block Development Committees, Halqa Panchayats) for rural self-governance.
  • District Development Councils (DDCs): Elected bodies at the district level — first elections held December 2020; provide a democratic layer at the district level following the reorganisation.
