⏳ Chronological History

Historical Timeline

Key milestones in Jammu & Kashmir's political and constitutional history — from the Dogra dynasty's founding to the Supreme Court's 2023 verdict.

Chronology

Milestones 1820–2023

Click any linked event to read the full article on that topic.

1820
Gulab Singh — Jammu Vassal

The Sikh Empire under Maharaja Ranjit Singh recognised Gulab Singh as the hereditary Raja of Jammu, beginning the Dogra dynasty's rise to prominence in the region.

📜 Read History →
1846
Treaty of Amritsar — Princely State Founded

After the First Anglo-Sikh War, the British East India Company transferred Kashmir to Gulab Singh via the Treaty of Amritsar, creating the princely state of Jammu and Kashmir — one of the largest in British India.

📜 Read History →
1925
Hari Singh Becomes Maharaja

Hari Singh succeeded his uncle Pratap Singh as Maharaja of Jammu and Kashmir. He would prove to be the last ruling Maharaja of the princely state.

👤 Read Biography →
1931
Political Unrest in Kashmir

Agitation against Dogra rule began, led by Sheikh Mohammad Abdullah and others. This marked the beginning of organised political activity in the Kashmir Valley.

📊 Read Analysis →
14–15 August 1947
Independence of India and Pakistan

The Partition of British India created two independent dominions. Princely states, including Jammu & Kashmir, were free to accede to either dominion or remain independent.

📜 Read History →
October 1947
Instrument of Accession Signed

Facing a Pakistan-supported tribal invasion, Maharaja Hari Singh signed the Instrument of Accession to India, ceding defence, external affairs, and communications. India sent military assistance. The accession was challenged by Pakistan, initiating the Kashmir conflict.

👤 Read about Hari Singh →
1948–1949
First Kashmir War & UN Involvement

The first Indo-Pakistan War over Kashmir ended with a UN-brokered ceasefire on 1 January 1949, establishing the Line of Control (then Ceasefire Line). A UN resolution called for a plebiscite that was never held.

🏛️ Read Politics →
1949
Article 370 Inserted in Indian Constitution

Article 370 was inserted into Part XXI of the Indian Constitution, granting Jammu & Kashmir special autonomous status — including a separate constitution, state flag, and limited applicability of central laws.

⚖️ Read Article 370 →
1952
Monarchy Abolished; Delhi Agreement

The Delhi Agreement defined the relationship between J&K and India further. Hari Singh was removed as titular head; Sheikh Abdullah was appointed Prime Minister of J&K.

👤 Read Biography →
1957
J&K Constituent Assembly Adopts State Constitution

J&K's own Constituent Assembly adopted the state's Constitution, which came into effect on 26 January 1957. The Assembly dissolved without recommending the abrogation of Article 370, making it effectively permanent.

⚖️ Read Article 370 →
1965 & 1971
Subsequent Indo-Pakistan Wars

The 1965 war saw fighting along the Kashmir border. The 1971 war, though focused on East Pakistan (Bangladesh), also involved the western front. The 1972 Simla Agreement established the Line of Control.

🏛️ Read Politics →
1987–1990s
Insurgency and Security Challenges

Disputed elections in 1987 and cross-border militancy led to a prolonged insurgency in the Kashmir Valley, with significant security and humanitarian consequences lasting through the 1990s and 2000s.

🏛️ Read Politics →
5–6 August 2019
Abrogation of Article 370

The Government of India issued Presidential Orders superseding the 1954 Order, making all provisions of the Indian Constitution applicable to J&K. Article 370 was effectively rendered inoperative (except clause 1).

⚖️ Read Article 370 →
31 October 2019
Reorganisation — Two Union Territories Created

The Jammu and Kashmir Reorganisation Act, 2019 came into effect. The state of J&K was bifurcated into two Union Territories: Jammu & Kashmir (with a legislative assembly) and Ladakh (without a legislative assembly).

📋 Read Administration →
11 December 2023
Supreme Court Upholds Abrogation

A five-judge Constitution Bench of the Supreme Court of India unanimously upheld the constitutionality of the 2019 abrogation of Article 370. All 23 petitions challenging the move were dismissed. The Court also directed elections to be held by September 2024.

⚖️ Read Article 370 →
Sept–Oct 2024
Legislative Assembly Elections Held

The first assembly elections in ten years were held across three phases, resulting in a legislative majority for the National Conference (NC) and Congress alliance. Omar Abdullah was sworn in as Chief Minister on October 16, 2024.

🏛️ Read Politics & Governance →
2026
Dual-Executive Governance Conflict

The elected NC government operates under expanded Transaction of Business Rules that give the centrally appointed Lieutenant Governor final authority over police, public order, and senior appointments, leading to ongoing administrative friction.
