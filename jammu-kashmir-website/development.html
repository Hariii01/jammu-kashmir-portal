📊 Development Analysis Overview

Development in Jammu & Kashmir is powered by a strategic mixture of the Central Government's Prime Minister’s Development Package (PMDP), the New Central Sector Scheme (NCSS), and local executions under the **Jammu and Kashmir Infrastructure Development Finance Corporation (JKIDFC)**. This tracker presents the physical ground realities of J&K's transition.

Phase I

Completed Achievements (Happened Already)

Key infrastructures, strategic tunnels, and smart corridors that are active and serving the public today.

✓ Active & Operational

🌉 The Chenab Arch & Anji Khad Railway Bridges

Objective: Overcoming extreme Himalayan geography to connect Kashmir Valley with the Indian Railways grid.

The completion of the world's highest railway arch bridge over the Chenab River (elevated 359 meters above the riverbed) alongside India's first cable-stayed bridge at Anji Khad. Track laying and trial runs are complete, paving the way for direct railway links between Srinagar and Jammu.

Source: USBRL Monitoring Cell / Northern Railways
✓ Active & Operational

🚇 Z-Morh Escape Tunnel & Banihal-Qazigund Double Tube

Objective: Year-round, all-weather vehicular connectivity through heavy snow boundaries.

The 8.4 km Banihal-Qazigund road tunnel is fully open, bypassing landslide-prone sectors of the NH-44 highway. Concurrently, the strategic 6.5 km Z-Morh Tunnel's escape tube has been completed, providing vital winter access between Srinagar and Sonamarg.

Source: National Highways and Infrastructure Development Corporation Ltd (NHIDCL)
✓ Active & Operational

⛲ Srinagar Jhelum Riverfront Rejuvenation

Objective: Modernizing urban mobility and heritage spaces in Srinagar district.

Under the Srinagar Smart City initiative, the Jhelum Riverfront and Polo View market have been converted into pedestrian-friendly, high-density heritage zones. The city has also introduced a fleet of green electric transit buses to lower carbon footprint levels.

Source: Srinagar Smart City Limited (SSCL)
Phase II

Ongoing Development (In Process)

Flagship projects currently under active excavation, construction, or trial phases.

⏳ Active Construction

🏔️ The Zojila Pass Tunnel Project

Objective: Connecting Srinagar Valley with Ladakh UT all-year round.

Excavation is actively underway on the 14.15 km Zojila Pass Tunnel. Positioned at an altitude of 11,578 feet, this will be Asia's longest bi-directional tunnel, reducing travel time across the hazardous Zojila pass from 3 hours down to just 15 minutes.

Source: National Highways and Infrastructure Development Corporation Ltd (NHIDCL)
⏳ Active Construction

🌊 Jammu Tawi Riverfront Development

Objective: Flood control and commercial zone expansion in Jammu city.

Modeled on successful riverfront formats across Western India, work is in progress to build artificial barrages, concrete channel walls, and recreational parks along the Tawi River bed, safeguarding low-lying city margins from monsoon overflows.

Source: Jammu Smart City Limited (JSCL) / My Jammu
⏳ Active Construction

⚡ Chenab Valley Hydropower Corridors (Pakal Dul & Kiru)

Objective: Harnessing run-of-the-river power potential for regional self-sufficiency.

Construction of the 1,000 MW Pakal Dul Hydroelectric Project and the 624 MW Kiru Hydroelectric Project on the Chenab River. These projects aim to expand power availability, although they face local ecological review constraints.

Source: Chenab Valley Power Projects (CVPPPL) / Ministry of Power
Phase III

Future Plans & Frameworks

Policy reviews, structural expansion roadmaps, and land reservation guidelines designed for J&K's future.

🔮 Planned Framework

🏭 New Industrial Policy & MSME Revival Code

Objective: Protecting local cottage industries while attracting structured mega-investments.

Under current drafting by a high-level government panel, the revised Industrial Policy seeks to bring sick MSME craft units on par with new projects for state incentives, while introducing self-certification rules to prevent bureaucratic delays.

Source: J&K Department of Industries & Commerce / FCIK Proposals
🔮 Planned Framework

🛣️ PMGSY-IV Connectivity Matrix

Objective: Standardizing road transport to remaining remote mountainous habitations.

The upcoming phase of the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY-IV) targets laying over 3,300 km of new all-weather rural roads, connecting isolated Himalayan valleys and preventing agricultural market blockages.

Source: J&K Public Works Department (PWD)

⚠️ Eco-Vulnerability Investigation

Jammu division Focus: Post-2019 Infrastructure Speed vs. Land Sinking

Since the reorganization of J&K in 2019, infrastructure project implementation has accelerated rapidly. However, the fragile young fold mountains of the Chenab Valley (Ramban, Doda, Kishtwar) are showing signs of severe geological fatigue, leading to unprecedented land subsidence (sinking) and landslide hazards.
