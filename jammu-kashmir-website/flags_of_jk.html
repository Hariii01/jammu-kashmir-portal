🏴 Vexillological Guide

Flags of Jammu & Kashmir

A comprehensive historical inventory of 50 official, dynastic, political, military, and religious flags used throughout the region's history.

🏛️ Chapter 1: Official State & Sovereign Flags (1846–Present)

The sovereign banners representing Jammu and Kashmir as a unified princely state, state, and union territory:

🇮🇳
1. National Flag of India (Tiranga)
Period: 1947–Present (Sole official flag since 2019)
The saffron, white, and green tricolor with the navy blue Ashoka Chakra. It has been used officially since the accession in 1947 and became J&K's sole official flag following the abrogation of Article 370.
⚙️
2. J&K State Flag (Three Stripes)
Period: 1952–2019 (Official State Flag)
A deep red flag featuring a white agricultural plough in the fly and three vertical white stripes in the hoist representing the three regions of Jammu, Kashmir, and Ladakh. Removed in August 2019.
⚙️
3. Transitional J&K State Flag
Period: 1939–1952 (Interim Design)
Adopted by the National Conference and later modified during the transition period. Featured the white agricultural plough on a red background but lacked the three vertical stripes.
☀️
4. Princely State Flag of J&K
Period: 1846–1939 (Maharajas' Era)
The official flag of the Princely State of Jammu and Kashmir under the Dogra dynasty, featuring a split diagonal pattern of red and saffron yellow.

👑 Chapter 2: Royal Standards & Ruler Ensigns (Dogra Era)

Personal banners representing the ruling Maharajas of the Dogra dynasty:

🦁 Royal Standard
5. Maharaja Gulab Singh's Standard
Period: 1846–1857
A deep crimson banner featuring a gold-embroidered lion rampart, symbolizing the military conquest and consolidation of the Dogra state.
☀️ Sun Standard
6. Maharaja Ranbir Singh's Standard
Period: 1857–1885
A saffron-yellow banner depicting a central sun motif (*Surya Vansha*), representing the mythological solar lineage of the Dogra Rajputs.
👑 Coat of Arms
7. Maharaja Pratap Singh's Standard
Period: 1885–1925
Featured the state coat of arms—depicting a shield flanked by soldiers and topped by a crown—against a deep crimson background.
☀️🦁
8. Maharaja Hari Singh's Standard
Period: 1925–1947
The final royal banner of the J&K princely state, featuring the sun and lion crest within a saffron border on a red field.

🗳️ Chapter 3: Regional Political Party Flags

Banners representing the key political movements and parties in J&K's contemporary history:

⚙️ JKNC
9. J&K National Conference (JKNC)
Active: 1932–Present
A white agricultural plough on a red background. The design served as the basis for the former J&K state flag.
🖋️ JKPDP
10. People's Democratic Party (JKPDP)
Active: 1999–Present
A green and red vertical split flag with a central pen and inkpot symbol, representing rural education and grassroots empowerment.
🤝 JKPC
11. J&K People's Conference (JKPC)
Active: 1978–Present
A solid blue flag featuring a white hand symbol, representing regional economic self-reliance.
🍎 Apni Party
12. J&K Apni Party
Active: 2020–Present
A red, white, and blue vertical tricolor representing the unity of J&K's diverse regions.
🚲 JKNPP
13. National Panthers Party (JKNPP)
Active: 1982–Present
A blue flag featuring a white bicycle symbol, advocating for democratic reforms and anti-corruption.
☪️ JKAAC
14. Awami Action Committee (JKAAC)
Active: 1964–Present
A green flag depicting a white crescent and a double-edged sword, representing regional political aspirations.
DPAP
15. Democratic Progressive Azad Party (DPAP)
Active: 2022–Present
A blue, white, and yellow vertical tricolor representing regional progress and unity.
🌾 JKANC
16. Awami National Conference (JKANC)
Active: 1984–Present
A red flag featuring a white plough, used by the faction led by Khalida Shah.
🪷 BJP J&K
17. BJP J&K Unit Flag
Active: 1980–Present
The saffron and green flag with a central white lotus symbol, representing national integration and cultural nationalism.
🖐️ INC J&K
18. Congress J&K Unit Flag
Active: 1947–Present
The national party's tricolor banner featuring a central white open hand, representing center-state cooperative federalism.
☭ CPI(M) J&K
19. CPI(M) J&K Unit Flag
Active: 1964–Present
A red banner depicting a white hammer and sickle, representing local industrial and agricultural labor unions.
☀️ DSS
20. Dogra Sadar Sabha Flag
Active: 1904–Present
A white flag with a central sun logo, representing the cultural interests of Jammu's Dogra community.

📜 Chapter 4: Historical Empires & Conquerors (J&K Region)

Flags and banners of the empires that historically administered parts of Jammu, Kashmir, and Ladakh:

☀️ Karkota
21. Karkota Dynasty Standard
Era: c. 625–855 CE
A golden banner depicting a central sun motif, used during Lalitaditya's military campaigns across India.
🪷 Utpala
22. Utpala Dynasty Standard
Era: c. 855–1003 CE
A deep red standard depicting a golden lotus, representing temple commissions and river works under Avantivarman.
👑 Shah Mir
23. Shah Mir Sultanate Banner
Era: c. 1339–1561 CE
A green crescent flag representing Islamic administration, trade development, and arts patronage under Budshah.
🦁☀️
24. Mughal Empire Standard (Fray)
Era: 1586–1751 CE (Mughal J&K)
A green banner depicting a golden lion and rising sun, representing direct Mughal integration of Kashmir.
Durrani
25. Durrani Empire Standard
Era: 1752–1819 CE (Afghan J&K)
A green and red horizontal split banner, used during Afghan administration in the Kashmir Valley.
☬
26. Sikh Empire Standard (Nishan Sahib)
Era: 1819–1846 CE (Sikh J&K)
A triangular saffron flag featuring a central weapon motif (*Khanda*), representing Maharaja Ranjit Singh's integration of the region.
🇬🇧 EIC
27. East India Company Flag
Era: 1846 (Treaty of Amritsar)
The Union Jack canton on a red-striped field, used during British negotiations surrounding the purchase of Kashmir.
👑 Union Jack
28. British Raj Flag (Union Jack)
Era: 1858–1947 CE (Suzerainty)
The red, white, and blue flag representing British suzerainty over the J&K princely state.
🦅 Kushan
29. Kushan Empire Standard
Era: c. 1st–3rd century CE
A gold banner depicting a central fire altar, representing Kanishka's administration of Kashmir.
🦅 Gupta
30. Gupta Empire Standard
Era: c. 4th–6th century CE
A dark red banner depicting a golden Garuda image, representing classical imperial influence.

🛡️ Chapter 5: Military, Police, & Civil Ensigns

Flags representing J&K's law enforcement, armed regiments, and civilian security organs:

⚔️ JK Police
31. J&K Police Flag
Active: 1873–Present
A deep navy blue flag featuring the J&K Police crest—crossed swords under the national emblem, representing regional law enforcement.
⚔️ Northern Command
32. Northern Command (Indian Army) Flag
Active: 1972–Present
A red and black split flag depicting a central golden eagle and sword, representing territorial defense structures in J&K.
⚔️ Chinar Corps
33. XV Corps (Chinar Corps) Flag
Active: 1916–Present
A black flag featuring a central golden chinar leaf, representing army operations in the Kashmir Valley.
🏰 JAK LI
34. J&K Light Infantry Regiment Flag
Active: 1948–Present
A red flag featuring the infantry's crossed rifles symbol, representing a regiment raised from local volunteers in 1947.
⚔️ JAK RIF
35. J&K Rifles Regiment Flag
Active: 1821–Present
A green flag representing the regiment originally raised by Maharaja Gulab Singh, now integrated into the Indian Army.
⛰️ Ladakh Scouts
36. Ladakh Scouts Regiment Flag
Active: 1963–Present
A blue flag representing the mountain regiment raised from native Ladakhi soldiers for border defense.
🚒 JK FES
37. J&K Fire & Emergency Services Flag
Active: 1947–Present
A red flag depicting crossed fire axes and the state emblem, representing fire defense forces.
⚔️ Armed Police
38. J&K Armed Police Flag
Active: 1947–Present
A green flag with a police emblem, used by the armed reserves during public order operations.
🛡️ White Knight
39. XVI Corps (White Knight) Flag
Active: 1972–Present
A red banner depicting a white knight symbol, representing army operations in the Jammu division.
NCC
40. J&K NCC Cadet Corps Flag
Active: 1948–Present
A red, blue, and green vertical tricolor representing youth cadet training operations in J&K.

🕉️ Chapter 6: Religious & Cultural Banners

Banners representing the religious, spiritual, and cultural heritage of J&K's communities:

☸️ Buddhist
41. Buddhist Five-Color Prayer Flag
Usage: Ancient–Present (Ladakh Region)
The blue, yellow, red, white, and orange flag representing the elements of Buddhist philosophy, used across Ladakh's monasteries.
☸️
42. Dharmachakra Flag
Usage: Monastic celebrations
A white flag featuring the golden wheel of law, representing Buddhist teachings across monasteries in Leh and Kargil.
☬
43. Khalsa Nishan Sahib
Usage: Gurudwara Banners
A saffron-colored triangular flag depicting a central weapons crest, used across Sikh places of worship in Jammu and Kashmir.
☪️ Alam
44. Shia Islamic Muharram Banner (Alam)
Usage: Shia mourning processions
Black and green religious banners carried during Muharram processions in Srinagar and Kargil.
🌙
45. Hazratbal Green Flag
Usage: Shrine assemblies
A green flag featuring a white crescent, representing Islamic spirituality at the Hazratbal Shrine in Srinagar.
🕉️ Sharda
46. Kashmiri Pandit Sharda Banner
Usage: Shaivite festivals
A golden flag depicting a trishul and damru, used during Herath (Shivratri) festivals by the Pandit community.
🦁 Vaishno Devi
47. Vaishno Devi Shrine Board Flag
Usage: Reasi Shrine admin
A white flag depicting a red trident and lion motif, representing the Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine Board in Katra.
⛰️ Amarnath
48. Amarnath Shrine Board Flag
Usage: Yatra coordination
A blue flag depicting a white mountain ice-lingam symbol, representing the SASB pilgrimage administration.
🕌 Sufi
49. Chrar-e-Sharief Sufi Banner
Usage: Sufi shrine urs
A green banner representing the spiritual order of Sheikh Noor-ud-Din Wali (Nund Rishi) at his shrine.
☸️ LBA
50. Ladakh Buddhist Association Flag
Usage: LBA social actions
A blue flag with a white wheel of law, representing the cultural interests of Ladakh's Buddhist community.
