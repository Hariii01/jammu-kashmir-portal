Tourism

Tourism — centered on Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam, and the Amarnath and Vaishno Devi shrines — is a major revenue source. Security conditions, infrastructure development, and seasonality significantly influence tourist arrivals.

Key Tourist Destinations

🛥️ Dal Lake, Srinagar

Iconic urban lake famous for houseboats, shikara rides, and floating gardens. Ecological concerns about encroachment and eutrophication are significant.

⛷️ Gulmarg

A premier ski destination in winter; one of Asia's highest gondola cable cars. Alpine meadow tourism in summer.

🏕️ Pahalgam

Valley resort town; base camp for Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage; meadow and river tourism.

🛕 Vaishno Devi

One of India's most visited pilgrimage sites (~8 million visitors per year); located in Reasi district of Jammu division.

⚠️ The Ecological & Social Toll of Mass Tourism

While tourism is a vital driver of J&K's GDP, the rapid expansion of unregulated mass tourism has created severe environmental and social pressures on the region's fragile ecosystems:

🌊 Water Pollution & Eutrophication

Dal and Wular lakes face severe eutrophication from untreated sewage discharged by houseboats, hotels, and floating residential setups. Runoff of phosphorus and nitrogen has caused rapid weed blooms, reducing dissolved oxygen and threatening native fish populations.

🏔️ Alpine Meadow & Soil Degradation

Gulmarg and Pahalgam have experienced unplanned concrete construction, leading to deforestation, soil compaction, and the loss of green pastures. The heavy movement of tourists and horses has accelerated trail erosion and damaged fragile alpine flora.

🚮 High-Altitude Waste Management Crisis

During seasonal pilgrimage seasons (like the Amarnath Yatra and Vaishno Devi trek), millions of visitors produce thousands of tons of plastic and organic waste. The lack of waste management infrastructure in high-altitude terrain leads to waste being dumped directly into streams, polluting the Lidder and Tawi rivers.

💧 Resource Strain & Local Inflation

The diversion of water and electricity to hotel zones during peak summer months creates artificial shortages for local residents. Additionally, commercial land-buying has driven up real estate prices, making housing less affordable for local families.

Handicrafts & Cottage Industries

The handicrafts sector is central to J&K's cultural economy and export earnings. Key products include:

  • Pashmina / Cashmere: Highest-value textile export; traditional hand-spun and hand-woven.
  • Hand-knotted carpets: International export market; labour-intensive luxury goods.
  • Woodcraft: Walnut wood carving, Khatamband (geometric ceiling panels).
  • Embroidery: Sozni (fine needle work on shawls), Aari (chain stitch on fabric).
  • Paper maché: Decorative objects; traditional Persian-origin craft.
