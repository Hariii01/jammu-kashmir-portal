What is the current constitutional status of Jammu & Kashmir?

Since 31 October 2019, Jammu & Kashmir is a Union Territory with a legislative assembly under Article 239A of the Constitution of India. This means it is directly administered under the central government (represented by a Lieutenant Governor appointed by the President), but has its own elected legislature and council of ministers. This is different from most Union Territories (like Chandigarh), which do not have legislatures, and also different from full states (like Punjab), which have governors rather than LGs and greater legislative autonomy.

What happened to Article 370?

Article 370 was effectively abrogated on 5–6 August 2019 via Presidential Orders that made all provisions of the Indian Constitution applicable to J&K, and rendered clauses 1(b) and 1(c) of Article 370 inoperative. The Jammu and Kashmir Reorganisation Act, 2019 (31 October 2019) then bifurcated the state into two Union Territories. On 11 December 2023, a five-judge Constitution Bench of the Supreme Court of India unanimously upheld the constitutionality of the abrogation. 23 petitions challenging the move were dismissed.

What is the Instrument of Accession?

The Instrument of Accession is the legal document signed by Maharaja Hari Singh on 26 October 1947, accepted by Governor-General Mountbatten on 27 October 1947, by which the princely state of Jammu and Kashmir acceded to the Dominion of India. It transferred defence, external affairs, and communications to India while leaving all other subjects with the state. Maharaja Hari Singh signed it in response to a Pakistan-backed tribal invasion and in exchange for Indian military assistance. It is the foundational legal document of J&K's relationship with India.

What are the capitals of Jammu & Kashmir?

Jammu & Kashmir has two capitals in a historical rotation: Srinagar is the summer capital (used from May to October) and Jammu is the winter capital (used from November to April). This system dates to the Dogra era when the Maharaja and the court would move between the hot Jammu plains and the cooler Kashmir Valley. The High Court also follows a similar pattern.

How many districts does J&K have?

The Union Territory of Jammu & Kashmir has 20 districts across 2 divisions:

  • Kashmir Division (10 districts): Srinagar, Ganderbal, Budgam, Anantnag, Kulgam, Pulwama, Shopian, Baramulla, Bandipora, Kupwara
  • Jammu Division (10 districts): Jammu, Samba, Kathua, Udhampur, Reasi, Rajouri, Poonch, Doda, Ramban, Kishtwar

Note: Ladakh, with its own 2 districts (Leh and Kargil), is a separate Union Territory since 31 October 2019.

Who was Maharaja Hari Singh?

Hari Singh (September 1895 – 26 April 1961) was the last ruling Maharaja of the princely state of Jammu and Kashmir. He was a member of the Dogra dynasty — Rajput rulers from the Shivalik hills — and ruled from 1925 to 1949. He is primarily remembered for signing the Instrument of Accession to India in October 1947, following a Pakistan-backed tribal invasion of his kingdom. His decision shaped the entire subsequent political history of the region. His son Karan Singh became the first constitutional head of state (Sadr-i-Riyasat) of the state of J&K.

What is the difference between J&K state, J&K UT, and the region of Kashmir?

These terms are often confused. Here's a quick breakdown:

  • J&K state: Existed from 1947 to 2019. Was a full state of India with its own governor, state legislature, and constitution.
  • J&K Union Territory (UT): Since 31 October 2019. The former state minus Ladakh. Has a Lieutenant Governor, an elected legislature, and is governed as a UT under central jurisdiction.
  • Ladakh UT: Carved out of the former state in 2019. A UT without a legislature, comprising Leh and Kargil districts.
  • Kashmir region (geographical): A broader region encompassing the J&K UT, Indian-administered parts, Pakistan-administered Azad Jammu & Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan, and Aksai Chin (administered by China).
What languages are spoken in J&K?

J&K is linguistically diverse. Major languages include:

  • Kashmiri — spoken mainly in the Kashmir Valley; scheduled language under the 8th Schedule
  • Dogri — spoken in the Jammu division; scheduled language under the 8th Schedule
  • Urdu — the primary official language; widely understood
  • Hindi — official; especially in the Jammu region
  • Pahari, Gojri — spoken by communities in Rajouri, Poonch, Gujjars, etc.

What is the economy of J&K based on?

The economy is primarily driven by:

  • Horticulture: Apple cultivation alone employs 3.5 million people and contributes approximately 10% of GSDP. Other products include saffron, walnuts, pears, and cherries.
  • Tourism: Srinagar (Dal Lake), Gulmarg, Pahalgam, and Vaishno Devi (Jammu) are major draws.
  • Handicrafts: Pashmina shawls, hand-knotted carpets, woodcraft, and embroidery are internationally recognised export industries.
  • Minerals: Kashmir Blue Sapphire, limestone, gypsum.
